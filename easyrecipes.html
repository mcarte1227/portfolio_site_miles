Easyrecipes

Responsive web application created with the focus of providing quick and hassle-free recipes to follow with minimal ingredients. The main objective of this project was learning about and going through the Lean UX method of conducting user research.


SKILLS USED

  •  User research
  •  User persona creation
  •  User flow diagrams
  •  Usability testing
  •  Rapid prototype sketching
  •  Low + Mid fidelity wireframes
  •  Mood board + Style guide
  •  User interface design
  •  Reaponsive layout design

INTRODUCTION

Easyrecipes is a responsive web-based application aimed to provide users who are busy throughout the day with a variety of simple recipes to follow that saves on time and ingredients. This way, they can find simple and quick recipes that caters to a variety of eating and life styles regardless of the amount of culinary experience they may or may not have. While going on Eaayrecipes, users will be able to search for simple and quick recipes of any category, save them to a list, add them to a custom meal planner, and follow along with text or video instructions.

The following hypothesis statement was then formulated to use as a foundation for my user research.

"Users with a busy and hectic schedule, new to cooking, or just want short and simple recipes to follow will be able to have a variety of options to choose from."

INSPIRATION + MOOD BOARD

After the UX process of collecting user information and deciding on the basic layout, it was time to move on to the visual design process.

I chose to aim for a more minimalistic layout approach with a warm, earthy color palette, as I felt a warmer color scheme would be a nice way to reflect a since of friendliness a recipe app ought to have.

After I had the aesthetic and layout I wanted, I went on to creating the style guide I would apply the the app.

CONCLUSION

  • The brief for this project was rather open ended, as it mainly just called for me to design a responsive web application for either a location-based or recipe site.
  • I decided on my features based on the types of responses I received from the initial user interview stage. From the frustrations and lack of specific functions given to me by those I interviewed, I did my best to offer a solution to solve those issues.
  • In terms of design challenges, I remembered hitting a roadblock when it came to the multiple rapid sketching. From the beginning, I already had a general layout idea in my mind I wanted to use, and having to sketch out multiple ideas one after the other proved to be quite difficult.
  • When I did come up with different layout ideas, I felt as though they were rather sparse and I was not coming up with as many distinct different ideas as I should have been. To combat this, after each set of crazy 8’s, I took a short break to try to clear my head. Then, I did manage to come up with a few new layout designs more frequently did I did before.
  • Overall, I believe I met the original objective of designing a responsive recipe web application. During the earlier phases of the process, I made sure to consult the responses I received from the interviews regarding what users would like to see in a recipe app when planning the layout.
